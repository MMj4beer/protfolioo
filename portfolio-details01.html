Project Explained

A DEEP LEARNING MODEL TO DETECT BI-RADS SCORE FROM A MAMMOGRAME

  • Category:Deep learning model
  • Client:Father Mullar Medical Collage , India
  • Project date: Aug, 2022

Breast cancer is a significant global health issue affecting millions of women worldwide. Mammography is a standard imaging modality used for breast cancer screening and diagnosis, and radiologists often rely on the Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System (BI-RADS) to classify breast lesions and determine the likelihood of cancer. However, interpreting mammograms can be challenging and time-consuming, and radiologists may have varying degrees of expertise and experience. This project aimed to develop a deep learning model capable of detecting the BI-RADS score from a mammogram, thereby reducing the workload of radiologists and providing faster and more accurate diagnoses. The deep learning model was trained on a large dataset of mammograms and achieved high sensitivity and specificity in detecting the BI-RADS score. The outcome of this project is a highly accurate and efficient deep learning model that can assist radiologists in making more accurate and consistent diagnoses, reducing the need for unnecessary biopsies, and improving patient outcomes. The advantages of this project include improving the quality and consistency of breast cancer diagnoses, reducing the workload of radiologists, and demonstrating the potential of deep learning in automating complex image analysis tasks in the field of radiology. In conclusion, this project represents a significant step forward in the early detection and diagnosis of breast cancer, and has the potential to save countless lives by providing faster and more accurate diagnoses, reducing unnecessary biopsies, and improving patient outcomes.
