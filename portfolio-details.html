Project Explained

AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM FOR MARKING INFERIOR ALVEOLAR NERVE AND TO DETECT MANDIBULAR CANAL FROM A CB-CT IMAGE

  • Category:An artificial intelligence system
  • Client:Father Mullar Medical Collage , India
  • Project date: March, 2023

The project aims to address a common challenge in dental implant treatment planning, which is the accurate identification of the inferior alveolar nerve and mandibular canal from a CB-CT image. These anatomical structures play a critical role in implant placement, and their inadvertent injury during surgery can lead to severe complications, including numbness, pain, and even paralysis. Currently, the identification of the nerve and canal is done manually by experienced clinicians, which is time-consuming, subjective, and prone to human error. To address this issue, the project proposes the development of an artificial intelligence system that can automatically mark the inferior alveolar nerve and detect the mandibular canal from a CB-CT image. The system uses a combination of machine learning algorithms, including deep learning neural networks, to identify the relevant structures and provide accurate and consistent results. The system is trained on a large dataset of CB-CT images and validated on a separate dataset to ensure its generalizability and reliability. The outcome of the project is a highly accurate and efficient artificial intelligence system capable of marking the inferior alveolar nerve and detecting the mandibular canal from a CB-CT image. This system has the potential to revolutionize dental implant treatment planning by reducing the risk of nerve and canal injury, improving surgical outcomes, and saving time for clinicians. It could also have broader implications for the field of radiology by demonstrating the potential of artificial intelligence in automating complex image analysis tasks and improving diagnostic accuracy.
