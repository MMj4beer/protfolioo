Project Explained

Brest Cancer dtection from Mammogram

  • Category:Deep learning model
  • Client:Father Mullar Medical Collage , India
  • Project date: Nov, 2021

Breast cancer is the most common cancer among women worldwide, and early detection is crucial for effective treatment and improved patient outcomes. However, traditional methods for detecting breast cancer, such as mammography, can be time-consuming and subject to interpretation by medical professionals. Moreover, the accuracy of mammography depends on the skills and experience of the radiologist, and even the most skilled practitioners may miss small or subtle signs of cancer. Deep learning models, particularly those based on CNNs, have shown great promise in improving the accuracy and efficiency of breast cancer detection. By analyzing large amounts of data and identifying complex patterns and features, these models can learn to identify the subtle signs of breast cancer that might be overlooked by human observers. This has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy of breast cancer diagnoses, leading to earlier detection and better patient outcomes. The deep learning model proposed in this project aims to improve upon the existing state-of-the-art by utilizing a large dataset of mammogram images to train a CNN-based algorithm that can distinguish between malignant and benign tumors. The model will be able to detect even subtle signs of breast cancer with high accuracy, leading to earlier detection and more successful treatment. One of the main benefits of a deep learning model for breast cancer detection is its ability to reduce the risk of human error. Unlike human observers, the model can analyze large amounts of data quickly and accurately, leading to more efficient and reliable diagnoses. Moreover, the model can provide a more objective assessment of mammogram images, reducing the potential for subjective interpretation by medical professionals. Ultimately, the deep learning model developed in this project has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and efficiency of breast cancer diagnoses, leading to earlier detection and better patient outcomes. By providing a more reliable and objective assessment of mammogram images, the model can reduce the workload for medical professionals and make breast cancer screening more accessible and affordable for patients.
